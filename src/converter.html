What is the difference between mmol/L and mg/dl.

Both sets of units and used to measure blood glucose (sugar) levels and both give the measurement of the concentration of glucose (sugar) in the blood just in different ways. mmol/L gives the molarity, which is the number of molecules of a substance within a specified volume, in this case within 1 litre. mg/dL gives the concentration by the ratio of weight to volume, in this case milligrams per decilitre.
